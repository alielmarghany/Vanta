The Problem

Healthcare data is broken.
Entirely.

Trillions of health data points are generated annually. None of them talk to each other. Clinicians make decisions on 3% of the available signal. We are leaving decades of healthy life on the table.

01

Data lives in 47 silos

The average patient's health data is fragmented across EHRs, labs, wearables, genomics providers, and insurance systems that cannot communicate. Integration attempts fail 89% of the time.

02

Interventions arrive too late

Chronic disease is diagnosed an average of 6.2 years after the biological signal first appears in data. VANTA's longitudinal engine detects these signals years before clinical presentation.

03

No unified identity layer

There is no universal health identity that persists across providers, time, and geography. Every care transition loses critical context. We built the identity primitive the industry is missing.

04

AI can't generalize

Health AI models trained on single-institution data fail catastrophically when deployed at scale. Without diverse, longitudinal, multi-modal training data, clinical AI is an expensive parlor trick.
